_Kotowaza_ (諺, ことわざ) are Japanese proverbs and sayings. Unlike Western
proverbs, _kotowaza_ can take many forms, such as _inarawashi_ (言い習わし), or
short sayings, which are the closest form to Western proverbs, _kanyouku_
(慣用句) or idiomatic phrases that have inexplicit meanings hidden behind the
literary ones, and the _yojijukugo_ (四字熟語) or four-character idioms.

Japanese culture is abundant with _kotowaza_, and they are frequently used in
daily life conversations, often shortened for the sake of simplicity.
