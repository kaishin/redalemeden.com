[Last time](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172516/http://neosamurai.blogspot.com/2006/05/kotowaza-vol1.html)
I mentioned the three types of _kotowaza_, based on their grammatical and
lexical constructions. Today I am going to dive into the magical realm of
_yojijukugo_.

_Yojijukugo_ are commonly known in English as "four-character compounds",
"four-character idioms", or "four-character idiomatic compounds". The idiomatic
meaning of the compound can't be directly inferred from the separate meanings of
every kanji in it. This said, it is wrong to believe that every _yojijukugo_ has
an idiomatic meaning, as there exists thousands of non-idiomatic four-kanji
compounds. _Yojijukugo_ were adopted from classical Chinese literature, Buddhist
literature and scriptures, old Japanese customs and proverbs, and historical and
contemporary Japanese life and social experience. (source Wiki)

Today's treat:

- 相手次第 (あいて しだい / _Aite shidai_)

Being up to the other party / Determining one's attitude or response based on
the character, attitude, or move(s) of the other party.

- 青田売買 (あおた ばいばい / _Aota baibai_)

Dealing in unharvested rice crop / One shouldn't go beyond the course of events,
and plan based on yet uncertain facts.

- 悪事千里 (あくじ せんり / _Akuji senri_)

Bad news travel fast / Ill news spread like wildfire.

- 悪木盗泉 (あくぼく とうせん / _Akuboku tousen_)

A virtuous person does not commit an act that offends his moral principles no
matter how hard pressed he may be.
