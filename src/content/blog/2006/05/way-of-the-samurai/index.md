_Bushido_ (武士道) is a code of conduct and way of life of the _samurai_ class
in feudal Japan. The word literally means "the way of the warrior."

Contrary to what many assume, _bushido_ isn't a written code. It's an ethical
legacy passed down orally or through the writings of notable warriors and
scholars. Pinning it down to a single definition doesn't quite do it justice. To
understand _bushido_, think of a strict, even stoic, self-discipline that places
certain values above everything else:

> 君に忠、親に孝、自らを節すること厳しく、下位の者に仁慈を以てし、敵には憐みをかけ、私欲を忌み、公正を尊び、富貴よりも名誉を以て貴しとなす

Which reads as:

> くんにちゅう、おやにこう、みずからをせっすることきびしく、かいのものにじんじをもってし、てきにはあわれみをかけ、しよくをいみ、こうせいをたっとび、ふうきよりもめいよをもってたっとしとなす。

"Faithfulness to one's master, filial piety, relentless self-discipline,
benevolence towards one's subordinates, pity towards one's enemies, self-denial,
esteem justice, honor above all." (approximate translation)

Tracing _bushido_'s origins is no easy task. It wasn't the work of a single
person or a group of scholars, but the essence of the _samurai_ class as it
unfolded through the centuries of war since the 11th century, coming into full
bloom in the first half of the 19th century.
