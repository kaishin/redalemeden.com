I had two things to write about today: art and soccer. But finding a way to
connect the two seemed unlikely. But then I stumbled upon [this
poster](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172131/http://www.picassomio.com/art/24884/en/)
by Tenmyouya Hisashi (天明屋尚), submitted to [an international art contest
organized in Germany for the 2006 World
Cup](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172131/http://www.artfootball.de/en/artists/),
and it brought everything together.

Japan's national team—sometimes called the Grandsons of Samurai abroad—didn't
fare so well in the tournament: a 1–3 defeat against Australia, a draw with
Croatia, and a final 1–4 loss to Brazil in their last chance to turn things
around.

As for Tenmyouya, he's a self-taught artist born in Tokyo in 1966, currently
based in Saitama. He blends different cultural elements using traditional
Japanese drawing techniques to depict a distorted image of Japan, often playing
on the stereotypes that foreigners hold. His works are packed with figurative
and symbolic details drawn from both modern and traditional cultures, and they
convey a sense of cultural fusion through heavy exposure to universal—and often
American—symbols. What makes them even more intriguing is their "sarcastic
disharmony": a dose of dark satire that leaves the viewer genuinely unsure what
to make of it all.
