The pairing of soccer and _samurai_ keeps showing up. Konami has launched a
[campaign site](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172638/http://www.konami.jp/we/samurai/)
for
[Winning Eleven 10](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172638/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winning_Eleven),
featuring a _samurai_ with the number 10 on his back, a nod to both the game's
version number and the jersey number traditionally worn by a team's top forward.

The campaign is titled ウイイレ侍がゆく！！ — "The Wiire Samurai Goes Forth!"

_Wiire_ ウイイレ (pronounced _wee-irei_) is the Japanese abbreviation of Winning
Eleven, written in full as ウイニングイレベン (_Uiningu Ireben_). Combine the
first two _katakana_ of each word and you get ウイイレ: _Uiire_, or _Wiire_. In
Japan, almost every long word gets abbreviated.
