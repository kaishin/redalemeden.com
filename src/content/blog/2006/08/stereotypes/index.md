If there's one thing I can't stand, it's cultural stereotypes. Every country
gets stereotyped, but Japan seems to get an especially large share of it.

[This article](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172136/http://www.indiana.edu/%7Ejapan/Digests/press-pfv.htm)
looks at how Japan and the Japanese are portrayed in American mainstream media.
As it notes, Japan is routinely misrepresented in the press, and when that's the
primary source of information for most people, the result is a distorted picture
that sticks. I've met people from all over the world carrying their own
assortment of unfounded stereotypes. There's
[a website](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172136/http://www.justiceforjapan.net.ms/)
that collects them (warning: offensive language).

The stereotypes also come in tiers. For some, Japan is only sushi and sumo. For
others it's anime and video games. For many, Japan and Japanese are mysterious
and fundamentally different from everyone else.

The internet has helped to some extent. Blogs and social platforms have started
to shift that image. But not everyone is ready to listen. Ignorance is
humanity's number one enemy.
