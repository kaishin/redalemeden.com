A friend sent me a track from
[Ape Escape](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172136/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ape_Escape)
— _Saru! Get you!_
([サルゲッチュ](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172136/http://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%B5%E3%83%AB%E3%82%B2%E3%83%83%E3%83%81%E3%83%A5)),
a game I loved back in PSX days. The one track that caught my attention was
called "Wabi-Sabi Wall."

_Wabi-sabi_ (侘寂) is one of those Japanese aesthetic concepts that resists
translation. The English Wikipedia defines it as "a comprehensive Japanese world
view centered on the acceptance of transience," a view similar to _Mono no Aware_
(物の哀れ), which describes awareness of the transience of things and a gentle
sadness at their passing. That similarity confused me, so I headed to the
[Japanese Wikipedia](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172136/http://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%8F%E3%81%B3%E3%83%BB%E3%81%95%E3%81%B3)
for a closer look.

In Japanese, the compound splits into two:

_Wabi_ is the noun from the verb _wabu_, meaning "an inferior state over a
perfect one": the humble, the plain, the simple. Not ideal in any conventional
sense, but valued for it.

「侘（わび、侘びとも）とは、動詞「わぶ」の名詞形で、その意味は「立派な状態に対する劣った状態」となる。転じては「粗末な様子」、あるいは「簡素な様子」を意味している。」

_Sabi_ is the noun from _sabu_, meaning the deterioration of things over time —
the patina, the rust, the wear. The idea is that the beauty created by age
deserves appreciation in itself.

「寂（さび、寂びとも）は動詞「さぶ」の名詞形で、本来は時間の経過によって劣化した様子（経年変化）を意味している。英語ではpatina（緑青）の美が類似のものとして挙げられ、緑青などが醸し出す雰囲気についてもpatinaと表現される。」

Taken together, _wabi-sabi_ is something like "the beauty of imperfection,
incompleteness, and impermanence." I tried my best to put it into words, and I'm
still not quite there, but that's probably the point. Japanese traditional
aesthetics (_biishiki_) are subtle by nature.
