The Japanese equivalent of "cast pearls before swine"
is 猫に小判 ([NEKO NI KOBAN](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.geocities.com/Tokyo/5623/06.wav)),
literally "coins to a cat"—don't offer something to someone who can't use it
properly.

A
[koban](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://image.space.rakuten.co.jp/lg01/21/0000136021/62/imgaa839445chvk3g.jpeg)
was an oval gold coin used during the Edo period. Since
[Pokemon Diamond and Pearl](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.pokemon.co.jp/game/ds/dp/index.html)
just came out in Japan, I couldn't think of a better image than
[Meowth](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meowth)—or
Nyasu in the original version. The coin on his forehead is actually a koban.

There's a closer equivalent to the English
phrase: 豚に真珠 ([BUTA NI SHINJU](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.geocities.com/Tokyo/5623/07.wav)),
"pearls to a pig."
