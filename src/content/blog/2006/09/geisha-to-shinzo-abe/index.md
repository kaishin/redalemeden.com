I had a geisha plate on my desk that gave me the idea to write about the topic
yesterday. For those who want a professional write-up,
[TimesOnline](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.timesonline.co.uk/article/0,,3-2325867,00.html)
had an article this morning. To clear up the confusion of different
apprenticeship levels, I found
[Geisha Immortal](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.immortalgeisha.com/index.html)—[their FAQ](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.immortalgeisha.com/faq_geisha.php)
explains the differences between _geisha_, _maiko_, and _geiko_ in detail.

---

Mozilla is running a campaign to promote Firefox in Japan. They created a cute
fox mascot, as expected in
[the land of kawaii](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kawaii),
and they're
[running a contest](https://web.archive.org/web/20170310023720/http://foxkeh.jp/)
to name it. Being a dedicated Firefox user, I couldn't help suggesting a
name: フォックシー (Foxy). (via
[Japan Probe](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.japanprobe.com/?p=458))

The Washington Post published
[an article](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/08/25/AR2006082501176_pf.html)
arguing that
[Japan needs nationalism](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/08/25/AR2006082501176_pf.html),
but not at the expense of freedom of expression. Shinzo Abe, the favored
successor to PM Junichiro Koizumi, has declared his intention to upgrade the
Defence Agency to full ministry status, amend education laws to boost
patriotism, and expand Japan's military presence overseas. (via
[The Hindu](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/holnus/003200609031551.htm))
