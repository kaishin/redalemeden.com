Being an internet denizen, I spend more than 60% of my free time surfing. Every
morning, before breakfast, I skim more than twenty Japan-related news websites
and blogs. I don't use social networking services much, though I have accounts
on most of them, including MySpace.

Which brings me to:
[Yoshiki](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoshiki)
— ex-drummer and pianist of the legendary _Visual Kei_ band X-JAPAN
(一番好きなバンドだよ！), now running a solo career in Los Angeles — has a
[MySpace account](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.myspace.com/yoshiki).

What prompted this was
[an article in Metropolis](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://metropolis.co.jp/tokyo/recent/feature.asp)
on the growing popularity of SNS in Japan: Mixi and other topic-focused
platforms. Internet trends have shifted dramatically since 2005. It's hard to
imagine the web now without
[Wikipedia](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page),
[YouTube](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page),
and [Flickr](https://web.archive.org/web/20061107172119/http://www.flickr.com/),
let alone blogs and podcasts. The world really has become a small village.
