After Amaterasu
[declared the five male Kami her own](/blog/2006/10/shinwa-susanowos-visit/),
Susanowo grew arrogant. He filled the ditches between Amaterasu's ricefields,
destroyed the divisions she had set up, and desecrated her new palace. Amaterasu
gave him the benefit of the doubt: he acted out of whim, not malice. But
Susanowo went further. He flayed a piebald horse and flung it through a hole in
the ceiling of the weaving hall where Amaterasu and her handmaidens were
working.

Amaterasu retreated into **Amano-iwato** (天岩戸, Heavenly-Rock-Dwelling) and
blocked the entrance with a great boulder. Being the goddess of the sun, her
seclusion plunged the universe into unbroken darkness.

The eight hundred _Kami_ gathered at the bank of the Tranquil River of Heaven
and deliberated. **Omoi-kane** (no-kami) 思兼神 (Thought-Includer) devised a
plan:

- He gathered the birds of darkness and set them singing.
- He had **Ishikoridome** forge an eight-foot mirror (**Yata-no-kagami** 八咫鏡)
  from heavenly iron and rock.
- He had **Tamanoya** string 500 Yasaka beads.
- He placed a great _Sakaki_ tree before the cave, hung the mirror in its middle
  branches, and draped it with the jewels and other offerings.
- He asked **Amenokoyane** to recite liturgies and **Amenota-jikarawo** to hide
  beside the entrance.
- Finally, **Ameno-uzume** (Heavenly-Alarming-Deity) adorned herself with leaves
  and plants, stepped onto an overturned tub, and began to dance, baring parts
  of herself. The assembled _Kami_ erupted in laughter.

The sound reached Amaterasu inside the cave. Curious, she peeked out: "Methought
that owing to my retirement the Plain of Heaven would be dark, and likewise the
Earth would all be dark: how then is it that Ameno-uzume makes merry, and that
likewise the eight hundred myriad Deities all laugh?"

"We rejoice and are glad because there is a Deity more illustrious than Thou,"
replied Ameno-uzume.

Amenokoyane and Futotama turned the mirror toward her, and she was dazzled by
her own reflected light. Amenota-jikarawo seized the moment and pulled her out:
"Thou must not go back further in than this!" Light returned to the world.

The eight hundred _Kami_ then punished Susanowo: they fined him, shaved his
beard, and pulled out his fingernails and toenails before expelling him.
