When
[Susanowo defeated Yamata no Orochi](/blog/2006/10/shinwa-orochi-the-eight-headed-snake/),
he went looking for a place to celebrate his marriage with _Kushinada-hime_. He
arrived in a land in Izumo province and declared:

「ここに来て、私の心はすがすがしい」 (_Koko ni kite, watashi no kokoro wa
sugasugashii_), translated as: "As I arrived here, my heart is refreshed."

That place was named **Suga**, from the adjective _sugasugashii_ (すがすがしい).
He built a palace there, now considered **Suga-jinja** shrine in Izumo. As
clouds rose around the palace, Susanowo composed the first poem in Japan: a
_Tanka_, 31 syllables following the 5-7-5-7-7 mora pattern.

「八雲立つ　出雲八重垣　妻ごみに　八重垣作る　その八重垣を」

(_Yakumo-tatsu_ / _Izumo Yaegaki_ / _Tsumagome-ni_ / _Yaegaki-tsukuru_ / _sono
Yaegaki-wo_)

"Many clouds arise. The manifold fence of the forth-issuing clouds Makes a
manifold fence For the spouses to be within. Oh! that manifold fence."

_Tanka_ are an older form of Japanese poetry than _Haiku_. Susanowo and
Kushinada-hime settled in Suga and had children together. Their first notable
offspring was **Ohona-muchi** (no-kami). Susanowo then designated Ashinazuchi
and Tenazuchi as masters of the palace, granting them the title _Inada no
Miya-nushi no Kami_.
