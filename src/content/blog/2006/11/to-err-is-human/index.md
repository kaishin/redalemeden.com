Tonight I am going over the _kotowaza_ (Japanese proverbs) that convey the
meaning of "anyone can make mistakes".

- 猿も木から落ちる (_Saru mo ki kara ochiru_): This one is a direct translation
  of the western "Even monkeys fall from trees", meaning that even experts make
  mistakes in their own fields.

- こうぼうも筆の誤り (_Koubou mo fude no ayamari_): Koubou is a buddhist priest
  who was famous for his calligraphy; the _kotowaza_ means literally "Even
  Koubou made mistakes with his brush".

- 河童も川流れ (_Kappa mo kawa nagare_): well this one is quite interesting, in
  the way that it is distinctively Japanese. It means literally "Even _Kappa_
  can be taken by the stream"... And since _Kappa_ is unique to Japanese
  culture, it is rather more appealing, from my stand point... but wait! what is
  a _Kappa_? I am too sleepy to write about Obakemono (huh?) stories, so I'll
