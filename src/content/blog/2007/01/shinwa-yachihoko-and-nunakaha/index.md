Now that I had taken a breather, it is time to resume my Shinwa chronicles. I
stopped in _Ookuninushi's_ arc (大国主), when he went back from the Land of
_Yomi_ and terrorized his 80 brothers as advised by _Susanowo_. He bore a child
with his new bride, _Suseri-bime_ (スセリビメ), and continued making the land,
that was halted since the death of Izanami.

_Yagami-hime_ (ヤガミ姫), the beautiful princess of Inaba who had an eye on
_Ookuninushi_ and accepted to marry him, was jealous of his official bride
_Suseri-bime_. She hence abducted their child and stuck it on a branch of a tree
then fled to her parents place, thus the baby was baptised _Kinomata (no-kami)_
木之又神, meaning "Tree-Fork-Deity". From that point on, _Ookuninushi_ was given
an alias of _Yachihoko_, an abbreviation of _Hachi-Sen-Hoko-Kami_ (八千矛神), or
"One thousand spear Kami".

Yachihoko packed and left for the land of Koshi, where an attractive maiden
called _Nunakaha-Hime_ ヌナカハヒメ (沼河比売) is rumored to dwell. When he
arrived he met her, and exchanged love poems. _Suseri-bime_ felt an immense
jealousy when her groom left her behind. He was aware of her grief, and
distressed, he composed a song on his way back from Izumo province to soothe
her. Once he uttered his well chosen words on the back of his horse in front of
her, she offered him a saké cup and sang, saying:

> "Oh! Thou, my dear Ookuninushi indeed, being a man, probably hast on the
> various islands that thou seest, and on every beach that thou lookest on, a
> wife like the young herbs. But as for me alas! being a woman, I have no man
> except thee; I have no spouse except thee."

**Notes:**

- Nunakawa-hime is deified in Nagawa Shrine, located in Itoigawa-shi, Niigata
  prefecture.
- Oonamuji → Ookuninushi → Yachihoko
