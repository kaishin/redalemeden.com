As you might or might not know, I am a big fan of Japanese independent music
scene, and about 75% of all the music I listen to is from amateur and
independent Japanese artists. In Japanese these artists are labeled _Otokei_
音系, and their music is referred to as _Otokei Doujin_ or _Doujin Ongaku_
同人音楽. Needless to say that Otokei produce and publish their music by
themselves, without any major label backing them up. In Japanese the concept of
_Doujin_ is used to refer to any independently published work, be it manga,
music or a literary work. _Doujin_ means "same person", and refers to people who
are sharing the "same" hobbies. _Doujinshi_, refers to magazines or comics
published by fans for fans, and so does _Otokei Doujin_ convey when talking
about music. Tokyo Dan, the man behind the
"[What's happening in Tokyo](https://web.archive.org/web/20070518233056/http://www.whitokyo.info/WHIT.htm)"
Podcast coined the term Jindies as an English alternative. For further details
check Wikipedia:
[English](https://web.archive.org/web/20070518233056/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dojin_music)
/
[Japanese](https://web.archive.org/web/20070518233056/http://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%9F%B3%E7%B3%BB%E5%90%8C%E4%BA%BA)

I do listen to virtually all musical genres, but I have a penchant for metal,
rock, instrumentals and game music. Today I picked 5 songs by a2c, the
co-founder of Mintjam, my favorite indie band ever.

Mintjam is a band that was formed by Setzer and a2c in April 2003. They started
as an amateur band offering their sounds on the Japanese indie community
[Muzie](https://web.archive.org/web/20070518233056/http://www.muzie.co.jp/), but
they brushed up their style and performance when Kya joined later in the same
year, to become a widely acknowledged indie band. Their activity grew more
wide-ranging when TERRA and A. Koga joined the band in December 2004, although
Koga left shortly after. In 2006, kya also left the band. The highly technical
play style, as well as the elaborate melodies are the selling points of Mintjam.
Even though their rearrangements are faithful to the original works, they
feature often lengthy melodic guitar solos and more elaborate rhythms. Two of
their most acclaimed original songs are "Rival" and "A Rude Performance", while
the covers of "Theme of Arcueid" and "Romancing SaGa 3: Last Battle" are perhaps
their most famous game music arrangements.
